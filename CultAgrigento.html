THE TRADITIONS OF AGRIGENTO

Agrigento, one of the most fascinating cities in Sicily, is famous for its thousand-year history, its art and its cultural heritage. The traditions of Agrigento, which have their roots in centuries of history, are the reflection of its culture, which combines religious devotion, folklore and a passion for popular celebrations. Here are five traditions that characterize Agrigento:
1. The Feast of San Calogero The Feast of San Calogero, which is celebrated on 18 and 19 July, is one of the most important religious festivals in Agrigento. San Calogero, the patron saint of the city, is venerated as a protector against illness. The festival is an explosion of popular devotion: the statue of the saint is carried in procession through the streets of Agrigento, accompanied by a flurry of songs, prayers and firecrackers. The tradition also includes the blessing of sick people, who go on a pilgrimage to receive the grace of the saint. A characteristic aspect of the festival is the "horse race", which symbolizes the triumphal entry of the saint into the city.
2. The Varia of Palagonia The Varia of Palagonia is one of the most evocative traditions of Agrigento, even if it takes its name from the small town of Palagonia, but involves the entire province. It is celebrated every August 15, during the feast of the Assumption of Mary. Tradition has it that a large statue of the Madonna, representing the Assumption, is carried on the shoulders of the faithful in a procession. But the most spectacular part is the moment in which the "Varia", a huge structure, is lifted and carried in a race that crosses the streets of the town, creating a unique collective energy.
3. The Sagra del Mandorlo in Fiore Every year, during the first week of March, the Sagra del Mandorlo in Fiore celebrates the blossoming of the almond trees, an event that marks the beginning of spring. Agrigento, together with the Valley of the Temples, hosts this festival that attracts thousands of visitors. The festival is a tribute to the natural beauty of the territory, with cultural and artistic events, folk music and traditional dances. The "dance of the peasants", performed with typical Sicilian clothes, and the historical parade are among the most anticipated moments of the event, which also celebrates multiculturalism and integration, with the participation of folk groups from all over the world.
4. The Procession of the Mysteries The Procession of the Mysteries is one of the most important religious traditions of Agrigento, which takes place every year on Good Friday. It is a long and evocative procession that accompanies the Dead Christ through the streets of the city, with the "mysteries", that is, the simulacra that represent scenes of the passion and death of Christ, carried on the shoulders of the faithful. During the route, which lasts all night, the streets are filled with songs, prayers and moments of great emotional intensity. The procession is a ritual that unites the Agrigento community in a deep religious meditation and a heartfelt respect for the traditions of Holy Week.
5. The Carnival of Sciacca Even though Sciacca is a nearby town, the Carnival of Sciacca is one of the most colorful and fun traditions in the province of Agrigento. It takes place every year between February and March and attracts visitors from all over Sicily and beyond. The peculiarity of this carnival is its parade of allegorical floats decorated with flowers, lights and masks, which travel through the streets of the city accompanied by music, dances and choirs. It is an event that celebrates tradition, but also the taste for social satire and popular creativity. The Carnival of Sciacca is also an opportunity to taste the typical sweets of the period, such as chiacchiere and cassate.
