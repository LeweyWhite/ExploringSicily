THE TRADITIONS OF MESSINA

Messina, one of the most fascinating and historic cities in Sicily, is rich in traditions that reflect its thousand-year-old past and its strong cultural identity. Messina's traditions are a mix of religious devotion, folklore and popular celebrations that involve the entire community. Here are five of the most important traditions in Messina:
1. The Feast of Sant'Antonio Abate The Feast of Sant'Antonio Abate, celebrated on January 17, is one of the most heartfelt religious traditions in Messina. Sant'Antonio Abate is the protector of animals, and the feast is particularly important for farmers and breeders. The faithful bring their animals to church to be blessed, and the city fills with stalls selling typical local products, such as cheeses, salami and sweets. The tradition is accompanied by a religious procession that passes through the streets of the city, combining devotion with the celebration of rural life.
2. The Procession of the Vara of Ferragosto The Procession of the Vara is one of the most evocative religious and folkloristic events in Messina. It takes place on August 15, during the feast of the Assumption. The vara, a large decorated structure that represents the Madonna Assunta, is carried on the shoulders of devotees in a long procession that crosses the streets of Messina. It is a moment of great popular and religious participation, with thousands of faithful who come together to pay homage to the Madonna. The procession is one of the most exciting and spectacular events of the Messina summer.
3. The Palio of Messina The Palio of Messina is a tradition that dates back to the Middle Ages and is an event that takes place every year on August 15. During the Palio, the city's neighborhoods compete in a series of competitions and challenges, including races, popular games and shows. The most famous race is the "Carriola", a race in which participants push wheelbarrows loaded with goods through the streets of the historic center. The Palio is an opportunity to relive the medieval history of Messina and to strengthen the sense of community between the neighborhoods.
4. The Feast of San Giovanni The Feast of San Giovanni is a deeply felt tradition in Messina, celebrated on June 24, in honor of San Giovanni Battista, the patron saint of the city. The festivity is characterized by religious and popular events, including a solemn procession that winds through the streets of the city, accompanied by songs and prayers. One of the most evocative traditions of the festival is the "gioco dei fuochi", a series of spectacular fireworks that light up the sky of Messina, creating an atmosphere of celebration and joy.
5. The Carnival of Messina The Carnival of Messina is one of the liveliest and most colorful traditions of the city, which takes place during the Carnival period, before the beginning of Lent. The city comes alive with floats, masks and parades of folk groups that pass through the main streets, creating an atmosphere of celebration and joy. The Carnival of Messina is also characterized by the traditional "masks" and "pupi" of papier-mâché, which are exhibited in a traveling exhibition. During this period, the streets fill with music, dance and shows, and the gastronomic traditions, such as chiacchiere and crostoli, are a must of the festival.
